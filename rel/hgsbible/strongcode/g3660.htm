  #3660 ὀμνύω omnuo {om-noo'-o}

 a prolonged form of a primary but obsolete omo, for which another
    prolonged form (omoo {om-o'-o}) is used in certain tenses;
    TDNT - 5:176,683; v

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to swear
 2) to affirm, promise, threaten, with an oath
 3) in swearing to call a person or thing as witness, to invoke, swear by

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

A prolonged form of a primary but obsolete word, ὄμω [[omo]], for which another prolonged form (ὀμόω [[omoo]], {om-o'-o}) is used in certain tenses. To swear, that is, take (or declare on) oath:—swear.
