  #1114 γόης goes {go'-ace}

 from goao (to wail); TDNT - 1:737,126; n m

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) a wailer, a howler
 2) a juggler, enchanter (because incantations used to be uttered
    in a kind of howl)
 3) a deceiver, imposter

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

From γοάω [[goao]] (to wail); properly a wizard (as muttering spells), that is, (by implication) an impostor:—seducer.
