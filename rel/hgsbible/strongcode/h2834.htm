  #2834 חָשַׂף chasaph {khaw-saf'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 766; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to strip, strip off, lay bare, make bare, draw out
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to strip off
        1a2) to strip, lay bare
        1a3) to draw (water), skim, take from the surface

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to strip off, that is, generally to make naked (for exertion or in disgrace), to drain away or bail up (a liquid):—make bare, clean, discover, draw out, take, uncover.
