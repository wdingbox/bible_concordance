  #6238 עָשַׁר `ashar {aw-shar'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 1714; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to be or become rich or wealthy, enrich, pretend to be rich
    1a) (Qal) to be or become rich
    1b) (Hiphil)
        1b1) to make rich
        1b2) to gain riches
    1c) (Hithpael) to enrich oneself, pretend to be rich

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; properly to accumulate; chiefly (specifically) to grow (causatively make) rich:—be (-come, en-, make, make self, wax) rich, make [1 Kings 22:48 margin]. See H6240.
