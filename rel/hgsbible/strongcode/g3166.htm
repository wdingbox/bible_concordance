  #3166 μεγαλαυχέω megalaucheo {meg-al-ow-kheh'-o}

 from a compound of G3173 and aucheo (to boast, akin to G0837 and G2744); TDNT - n/a; v

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to be grandiloquent
 2) to boast great things, to bear one's self loftily in speech or action

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

From a compound of G3173 and αὐχέω [[aucheo]] (to boast; akin to G0837 and G2744); to talk big, that is, be grandiloquent (arrogant, egotistic):—boast great things.
