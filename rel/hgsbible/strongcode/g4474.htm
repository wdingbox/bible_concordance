  #4474 ῥαπίζω rhapizo {hrap-id'-zo}

 from a derivative of a primary rhepo (to let fall, "rap"); TDNT - n/a; v

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to smite with a rod or staff
 2) to smite in the face with the palm of the hand, to box the ear

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

From a derivative of ῥέπω [[rhepo]], a primary word, (to let fall, "rap"); to slap (with the palm of the hand):—smite (with the palm of the hand). Compare G5180.
