  #7911 שָׁכַח shakach {shaw-kakh'}
     or שָׁכֵחַ shakeach {shaw-kay'-akh}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 2383; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to forget, ignore, wither
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to forget
        1a2) to cease to care
    1b) (Niphal) to be forgotten
    1c) (Piel) to cause to forget
    1d) (Hiphil) to make or cause to forget
    1e) (Hithpael) to be forgotten

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to mislay, that is, to be oblivious of, from want of memory or attention:— X at all, (cause to) forget.
