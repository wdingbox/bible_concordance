  #0024 אָבִיב 'abiyb {aw-beeb'}

 from an unused root (meaning to be tender); TWOT - 1b; n m

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) fresh, young barley ears, barley
 2) month of ear-forming, of greening of crop, of growing green
    Abib, month of exodus and passover (March or April)

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

From an unused root (meaning to be tender); green, that is a young ear of grain; hence the name of the month Abib or Nisan:—Abib, ear, green ears of corn.

—Strong's (Hebrew & Chaldee Dictionary of the Old Testament)
