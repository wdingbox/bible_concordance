  #2821 חָשַׁךְ chashak {khaw-shak'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 769; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to be or become dark, grow dim, be darkened, be black, be hidden
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to be or grow dark
        1a2) to have a dark colour
        1a3) to grow dim
    1b) (Hiphil)
        1b1) to make dark, cause to be dark
        1b2) to hide, conceal
        1b3) to obscure, confuse (fig.)

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to be dark (as withholding light); transitively to darken:—be black, be (make) dark, darken, cause darkness, be dim, hide.
