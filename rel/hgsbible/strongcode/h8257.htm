  #8257 שָׁקַע shaqa` {shaw-kah'} (abbrev. Am 8:8)

 a primitive root; TWOT - 2456; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to sink, sink down, subside
    1a) (Qal) to sink
    1b) (Niphal) to sink, collapse
    1c) (Hiphil) to sink down, cause to settle

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

(Abbreviated in Amos 8:8); a primitive root; to subside; by implication to be overflowed, cease; causatively to abate, subdue:—make deep, let down, drown, quench, sink.
