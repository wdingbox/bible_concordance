  #4237 πρασιά prasia {pras-ee-ah'}

 perhaps from prason (a leek, and so an onion-patch); TDNT - n/a; n f

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) a plot of ground, a garden bed
 2) Hebrew idiom i.e. they reclined in ranks or divisions, so that several
    ranks formed, as it were separate plots

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

Perhaps from πράσον [[prason]] (a leek, and so an onion patch); a garden plot, that is, (by implication of regular beds) a row (repeated in plural by Hebraism to indicate an arrangement):—in ranks.

—Strong's (Greek Dictionary of the New Testament)
