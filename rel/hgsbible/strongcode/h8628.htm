  #8628 תָּקַע taqa` {taw-kah'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 2541; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to blow, clap, strike, sound, thrust, give a blow, blast
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to thrust, drive (of weapon)
        1a2) to give a blast, give a blow
        1a3) to strike or clap hands
    1b) (Niphal)
        1b1) to be blown, blast (of horn)
        1b2) to strike or pledge oneself

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to clatter, that is, slap (the hands together), clang (an instrument); by analogy to drive (a nail or tent pin, a dart, etc.); by implication to become bondsman (by handclasping):—blow ([a trumpet]), cast, clap, fasten, pitch [tent], smite, sound, strike, X suretiship, thrust.
