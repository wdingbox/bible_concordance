  #8407 תִּלְּגַת פִּלְאֶסֶר Tiglath Pil'ecer {tig-lath' pil-eh'-ser}
     or תִּלְּגַת פְּלֶסֶר Tiglath P@lecer {tig-lath' pel-eh'-ser}
     or תִּלְּגַת פִּלְנְאֶסֶר Tilgath Piln@'ecer {til-gath' pil-neh-eh'-ser}
     or תִּלְּגַת פִּלְנֶסֶר Tilgath Pilnecer {til-gath' pil-neh'-ser}

 of foreign derivation; TWOT - n/a; n pr m

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 Tiglath-pileser or Tilgath-pilneser = "thou will uncover the
     wonderful bond"
 1) an Assyrian king who attacked Samaria or northern kingdom of Israel
    in the reign of Pekah

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)
