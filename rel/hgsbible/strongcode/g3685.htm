  #3685 ὀνίνημι oninemi {on-in'-ay-mee}

 a prolonged form of an apparently primary verb (onomai, to slur),
    for which another prolonged form (onao) is used as an
    alternate in some tenses [unless indeed it be identical with
    the base of G3686 through the idea of notoriety]; TDNT - n/a; v

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to be useful, to profit, help
 2) to receive profit or advantage, be helped [or have joy]

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

A prolonged form of an apparent primary verb ὄνομαι [[onomai]], to slur; for which another prolonged form ὀνάω [[onao]] is used as an alternate in some tenses (unless indeed it be identical with the base of G3686 through the idea of notoriety); to gratify, that is, (middle voice) to derive pleasure or advantage from:—have joy.
