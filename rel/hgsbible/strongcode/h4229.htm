  #4229 מָחָה machah {maw-khaw'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 1178,1179,1181c; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to wipe, wipe out
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to wipe
        1a2) to blot out, obliterate
        1a3) to blot out, exterminate
    1b) (Niphal)
        1b1) to be wiped out
        1b2) to be blotted out
        1b3) to be exterminated
    1c) (Hiphil) to blot out (from memory)
 2) (Qal) to strike
 3) (Pual) full of marrow (participle)

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; properly to stroke or rub; by implication to erase; also to smooth (as if with oil), that is, grease or make fat; also to touch, that is, reach to:—abolish, blot out, destroy, full of marrow, put out, reach unto, X utterly, wipe (away, out).
