  #5324 נָצַב natsab {naw-tsab'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 1398; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to stand, take one's stand, stand upright, be set (over), establish
    1a) (Niphal)
        1a1) to station oneself, take one's stand
        1a2) to stand, be stationed
        1a3) to stand, take an upright position
        1a4) to be stationed, be appointed
        1a5) deputy, prefect, appointed, deputed (subst)
        1a6) to stand firm
    1b) (Hiphil)
        1b1) to station, set
        1b2) to set up, erect
        1b3) to cause to stand erect
        1b4) to fix, establish
    1c) (Hophal) to be fixed, be determined, be stationary

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to station, in various applications (literally or figuratively):—appointed, deputy, erect, establish, X Huzzah [by mistake for a proper name], lay, officer, pillar, present, rear up, set (over, up), settle, sharpen, stablish, (make to) stand (-ing, still, up, upright), best state.
