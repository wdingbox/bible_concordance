  #6186 עָרַךְ `arak {aw-rak'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 1694; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to arrange, set or put or lay in order, set in array, prepare, order,
    ordain, handle, furnish, esteem, equal, direct, compare
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to arrange or set or lay in order, arrange, state in order, set
             forth (a legal case), set in place
        1a2) to compare, be comparable
 2) (Hiphil) to value, tax

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to set in a row, that is, arrange, put in order (in a very wide variety of applications):—put (set) (the battle, self) in array, compare, direct, equal, esteem, estimate, expert [in war], furnish, handle, join [battle], ordain, (lay, put, reckon up, set) (in) order, prepare, tax, value.
