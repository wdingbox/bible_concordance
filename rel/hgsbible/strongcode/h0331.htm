  #0331 אָטַם 'atam {aw-tam'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 73; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to shut, shut up, close
    1a) (Qal) to shut, stop (of lips)
    1b) (Hiphil) of the wicked stopping their ears (fig.)

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to close (the lips or ears); by analogy to contract (a window by bevelled jambs):—narrow, shut, stop.
