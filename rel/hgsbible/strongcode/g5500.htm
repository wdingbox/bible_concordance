  #5500 χειροτονέω cheirotoneo {khi-rot-on-eh'-o}

 from a comparative of G5495 and teino (to stretch); TDNT - 9:437,1309; v

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to vote by stretching out the hand
 2) to create or appoint by vote: one to have charge of some
    office or duty
 3) to elect, create, appoint

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

From a compound of G5495 and τείνω [[teino]] (to stretch); to be a hand reacher or voter (by raising the hand), that is, (genitive) to select or appoint:—choose, ordain.
