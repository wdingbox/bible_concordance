  #1580 גָּמַל gamal {gaw-mal'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 360; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to deal fully with, recompense
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to deal out to, do to
        1a2) to deal bountifully with
        1a3) to recompense, repay, requite
 2) (Qal) to wean a child
    (Niphal) to be weaned
 3) (Qal) to ripen, bear ripe (almonds)

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to treat a person (well or ill), that is, benefit or requite; by implication (of toil) to ripen, that is, (specifically) to wean:—bestow on, deal bountifully, do (good), recompense, requite, reward, ripen, + serve, wean, yield.
