  #4603 מָעַל ma`al {maw-al'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 1230; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to act unfaithfully, act treacherously, transgress, commit a trespass
    1a) (Qal) to act unfaithfully or treacherously
        1a1) against man
        1a2) against God
        1a3) against devoted thing
        1a4) against husband

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; properly to cover up; used only figuratively to act covertly, that is, treacherously:—transgress, (commit, do a) tresspass (-ing).
