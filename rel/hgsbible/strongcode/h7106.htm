  #7106 קָצַע qatsa` {kaw-tsah'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 2056,2057; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to scrape, scrape off, cut off
    1a) (Hiphil) to scrape
 2) to be cornered, be set in corners
    2a) (Hophal) to cut off places, make corners

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to strip off, that is, (partially) scrape; by implication to segregate (as an angle):—cause to scrape, corner.
