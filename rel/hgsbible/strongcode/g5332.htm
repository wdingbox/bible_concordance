  #5332 φαρμακεύς pharmakeus {far-mak-yoos'}

 from pharmakon (a drug, i.e. spell-giving potion); TDNT - n/a; n m

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) one who prepares or uses magical remedies
 2) sorcerer

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

From φάρμακον [[pharmakon]] (a drug, that is, spell giving potion); a druggist ("pharmacist") or poisoner, that is, (by extension) a magician:—sorcerer.
