  #0029 אֲבִיָּה 'Abiyah {ab-ee-yaw'}
     or prolonged אֲבִיָּהוּ 'Abiyahuw {ab-ee-yaw'-hoo}

 from H0001 and H3050; TWOT - n/a; n pr m

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 Abia or Abiah or Abijah = "Jehovah is (my) father"
 1) king of Judah, son and successor of Rehoboam
 2) second son of Samuel
 3) son of Jeroboam the first, king of Israel
 4) son of Becher, a Benjamite
 5) head of a priestly house (one of the 24 Levite groups)
 6) head of a priestly house (after the exile)
 7) wife of Hezron
 8) mother of Hezekiah (cf H0021)

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

From H0001 and H3050; father (that is worshipper) of Jah; Abijah, the name of several Israelite men and two Israelitesses:—Abiah, Abijah.
