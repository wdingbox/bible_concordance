  #1129 בָּנָה banah {baw-naw'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 255; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to build, rebuild, establish, cause to continue
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to build, rebuild
        1a2) to build a house (ie, establish a family)
    1b) (Niphal)
        1b1) to be built
        1b2) to be rebuilt
        1b3) established (of restored exiles) (fig.)
        1b4) established (made permanent)
        1b5) to be built up (of childless wife becoming the mother
             of a family through the children of a concubine)

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to build (literally and figuratively):—(begin to) build (-er), obtain children, make, repair, set (up), X surely.
