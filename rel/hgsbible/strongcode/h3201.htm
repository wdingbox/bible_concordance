  #3201 יָכֹל yakol {yaw-kole'}
     or (fuller) יָכוֹל yakowl {yaw-kole'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 866; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to prevail, overcome, endure, have power, be able
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to be able, be able to gain or accomplish, be able to endure,
             be able to reach
        1a2) to prevail, prevail over or against, overcome, be victor
        1a3) to have ability, have strength

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to be able, literally (can, could) or morally (may, might):—be able, any at all (ways), attain, can (away with, [-not]), could, endure, might, overcome, have power, prevail, still, suffer.
