  #4127 מוּג muwg {moog}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 1156; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to melt, cause to melt
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to melt, faint
        1a2) to cause to melt
    1b) (Niphal) to melt away
    1c) (Polel) to soften, dissolve, dissipate
    1d) (Hithpolel) to melt, flow

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to melt, that is, literally (to soften, flow down, disappear), or figuratively (to fear, faint):—consume, dissolve, (be) faint (-hearted), melt (away), make soft.
