  #7231 רָבַב rabab {raw-bab'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 2099; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to be or become many, be or become much, be or become great
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to be or become many
        1a2) to be or become great
        1a3) to be long (of journey)
 2) (Pual) ten thousands

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; properly to cast together (compare H7241), that is, increase, especially in number; also (as denominative from H7233) to multiply by the myriad:—increase, be many (-ifold), be more, multiply, ten thousands.

—Strong's (Hebrew & Chaldee Dictionary of the Old Testament)
