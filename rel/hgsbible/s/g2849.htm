  #2849 κολάζω kolazo {kol-ad'-zo}

 from kolos (dwarf); TDNT - 3:814,451; v

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to lop or prune, as trees and wings
 2) to curb, check, restrain
 3) to chastise, correct, punishment
 4) to cause to be punished

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

From κόλος [[kolos]] (dwarf); properly to curtail, that is, (figuratively) to chastise (or reserve for infliction):—punish.
