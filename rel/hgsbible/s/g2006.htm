  #2006 ἐπιτήδειος epitedeios {ep-ee-tay'-di-os}

 from epitedes (enough); TDNT - n/a; adj

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) fit, suitable, convenient, advantageous
 2) needful, esp. of the necessities of life

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

From ἐπιτηδές [[epitedes]] (enough); serviceable, that is, (by implication) requisite:—things which are needful.
