  #7136 קָרָה qarah {kaw-raw'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 2068,2068e; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to encounter, meet, befall, happen, come to meet
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to encounter, meet
        1a2) to befall
    1b) (Niphal)
        1b1) to encounter, meet (without pre-arrangement)
        1b2) to chance to be present
        1b3) to come to meet
    1c) (Hiphil) to cause to meet, appoint
 2) to build with beams
    2a) (Piel) to lay the beams of, furnish with beams

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to light upon (chiefly by accident); causatively to bring about; specifically to impose timbers (for roof or floor):—appoint, lay (make) beams, befall, bring, come (to pass unto), floor, [hap] was, happen (unto), meet, send good speed.
