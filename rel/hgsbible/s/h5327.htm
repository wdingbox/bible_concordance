  #5327 נָצָה natsah {naw-tsaw'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 1399,1400,1401; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) (Qal) to fly
 2) to struggle
    2a) (Niphal) to struggle
    2b) (Hiphil) to struggle
 3) to strip off, make desolate, fall in ruins
    3a) (Qal) to fall in ruins
    3b) (Niphal) desolated, ruined heaps (participle)

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; properly to go forth, that is, (by implication) to be expelled, and (consequently) desolate; causatively to lay waste; also (specifically), to quarrel:—be laid waste, ruinous, strive (together).
