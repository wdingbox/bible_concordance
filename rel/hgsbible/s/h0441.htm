  #0441 אַלּוּף 'alluwph {al-loof'}
     or (shortened) אלֻּף 'alluph {al-loof'}

 from H0502; TWOT - 109b; adj m

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) tame, docile
 2) friend, intimate
 3) chief

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

From H0502; familiar; a friend, also gentle; hence a bullock (as being tame; applied, although masculine, to a cow); and so a chieftain (as notable like neat cattle):—captain, duke, (chief) friend, governor, guide, ox.
