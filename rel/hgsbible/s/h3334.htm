  #3334 יָצַר yatsar {yaw-tsar'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 1973; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to bind, be distressed, be in distress, be cramped, be narrow, be
    scant, be in straits, make narrow, cause distress, beseige
    1a) (Qal) to suffer distress

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to press (intransitively), that is, be narrow; figuratively be in distress:—be distressed, be narrow, be straitened (in straits), be vexed.

—Strong's (Hebrew & Chaldee Dictionary of the Old Testament)
