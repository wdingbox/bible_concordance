  #8259 שָׁקַף shaqaph {shaw-kaf'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 2457; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to overlook, look down or out, overhang, look out and down
    1a) (Niphal) to lean over (and look), look down
    1b) (Hiphil) to look down, look down upon

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; properly to lean out (of a window), that is, (by implication) peep or gaze (passively be a spectacle):—appear, look (down, forth, out).

—Strong's (Hebrew & Chaldee Dictionary of the Old Testament)
