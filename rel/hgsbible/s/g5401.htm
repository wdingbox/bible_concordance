  #5401 φόβος phobos {fob'-os}

 from a primary phebomai (to be put in fear); TDNT - 9:189,1272; n m

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) fear, dread, terror
    1a) that which strikes terror
 2) reverence for one's husband

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

From a primary φέβομαι [[phebomai]] (to be put in fear); alarm or fright:—be afraid, + exceedingly, fear, terror.
