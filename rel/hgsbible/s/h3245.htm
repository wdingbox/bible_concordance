  #3245 יָסַד yacad {yaw-sad'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 875; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to found, fix, establish, lay foundation
    1a) (Qal) to found, establish, begin
    1b) (Niphal)
        1b1) to fix or seat themselves close together, sit in conclave
        1b2) to be founded
    1c) (Piel)
        1c1) to found
        1c2) to establish, appoint, ordain
    1d) (Pual) to be founded, be laid
    1e) (Hophal) to be founded

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to set (literally or figuratively); intensively to found; reflexively to sit down together, that is, settle, consult:—appoint, take counsel, establish, (lay the, lay for a) found (-ation), instruct, lay, ordain, set, X sure.
