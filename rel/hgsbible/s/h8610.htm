  #8610 תָּפַשׂ taphas {taw-fas'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 2538; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to catch, handle, lay hold, take hold of, seize, wield
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to lay hold of, seize, arrest, catch
        1a2) to grasp (in order to) wield, wield, use skilfully
    1b) (Niphal) to be seized, be arrested, be caught, be taken, captured
    1c) (Piel) to catch, grasp (with the hands)

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

A primitive root; to manipulate, that is, seize; chiefly to capture, wield; specifically to overlay; figuratively to use unwarrantably:—catch, handle, (lay, take) hold (on, over), stop, X surely, surprise, take.
