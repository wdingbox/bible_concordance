  #4445 מַלְכָּם Malkam {mal-kawm'}
     or מִלְכּוֹם Milkowm {mil-kome'}

 from H4428 for H4432; TWOT - n/a; n pr m

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 Milcom = "great king"
 1) the god of the Ammonites and Phoenicians to whom some Israelites
    sacrificed their infants in the valley of Hinnom
    1a) also 'Molech'
 2) a Benjamite, son of Shaharaim by his wife Hodesh

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)
