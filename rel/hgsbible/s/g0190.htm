  #0190 ἀκολουθέω akoloutheo {ak-ol-oo-theh'-o}

 from G0001 (as a particle of union) and keleuthos (a road);
    TDNT - 1:210,33; v

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to follow one who precedes, join him as his attendant,
    accompany him
 2) to join one as a disciple, become or be his disciple
    2a) side with his party

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

From G0001 (as a particle of union) and κέλευθος [[keleuthos]] (a road); properly to be in the same way with, that is, to accompany (specifically as a disciple):—follow, reach.
