  #0203 ἀκροβυστία akrobustia {ak-rob-oos-tee'-ah}

 from G0206 and probably a modified form of posthe (the penis or
    male sexual organ); TDNT - 1:225,36; n f

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) having the foreskin, uncircumcised
 2) a Gentile
 3) a condition in which the corrupt desires rooted in the flesh
    were not yet extinct

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

From G0206 and probably a modified form of πόσθη [[posthe]] (the penis or male sexual organ); the prepuce; by implication an uncircumcised (that is, gentile, figuratively unregenerate) state or person:—not circumcised, uncircumcised [with G2192], uncircumcision.
