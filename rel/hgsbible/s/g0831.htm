  #0831 αὐθεντέω authenteo {ow-then-teh'-o}

 from a compound of G0846 and an obsolete hentes (a worker); TDNT - n/a; v

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) one who with his own hands kills another or himself
 2) one who acts on his own authority, autocratic
 3) an absolute master
 4) to govern, exercise dominion over one

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

From a compound of G0846 and ἕντης [[hentes]] (obsolete; a worker); to act of oneself, that is, (figuratively) dominate:—usurp authority over.
