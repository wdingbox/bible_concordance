  #1380 δοκέω dokeo {dok-eh'-o}

 a prolonged form of a primary verb, doko {dok'-o} (used only in
    an alternate in certain tenses; cf the base of G1166) of
    the same meaning; TDNT - 2:232,178; v

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to be of opinion, think, suppose
 2) to seem, to be accounted, reputed
 3) it seems to me
    3a) I think, judge: thus in question
    3b) it seems good to, pleased me, I determined

 For Synonyms see entry G5837

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

A prolonged form of a primary verb δόκω [[doko]], {dok'-o} (used only as an alternate in certain tenses; compare the base of G1166); of the same meaning; to think; by implication to seem (truthfully or uncertainly):—be accounted, (of own) please (-ure), be of reputation, seem (good), suppose, think, trow.
