  #7951 שָׁלָה shalah {shaw-law'}
     or שָׁלַו shalav (Job 3:26) {shaw-lav'}

 a primitive root; TWOT - 2392; v

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to be at rest, prosper, be quiet, be at ease
    1a) (Qal)
        1a1) to be or have quiet
        1a2) to be at ease, prosper

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

The second form being used in Job 3:26; a primitive root; to be tranquil, that is, secure or successful:—be happy, prosper, be in safety.
