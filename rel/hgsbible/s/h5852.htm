  #5852 עֲטָרוֹת `Atarowth {at-aw-roth'}
     or עֲטָרֹת `Ataroth {at-aw-roth'}

 pl of H5850; TWOT - n/a; n pr loc

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 Ataroth = "crowns"
 1) a town east of the Jordan in Gilead, taken and built by the tribe
    of Gad
 2) a place on the boundary of Ephraim and Manasseh
 3) a place between Ephraim and Benjamin

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

Plural of H5850; Ataroth, the name (thus simply) of two places in Palestine:—Ataroth.
