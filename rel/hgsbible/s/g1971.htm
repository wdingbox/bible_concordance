  #1971 ἐπιποθέω epipotheo {ep-ee-poth-eh'-o}

 from G1909 and potheo (to yearn); TDNT - n/a; v

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to long for, desire
 2) to pursue with love, to long after
 3) to lust, harbour forbidden desire

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

From G1909 and ποθέω [[potheo]] (to yearn); to dote upon, that is, intensely crave possession (lawfully or wrongfully):—(earnestly) desire (greatly), (greatly) long (after), lust.
