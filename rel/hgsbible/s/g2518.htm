  #2518 καθεύδω katheudo {kath-yoo'-do}

 from G2596 and heudo (to sleep); TDNT - 3:431,384; v

—Greek Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) to fall asleep, drop off to sleep
 2) to sleep
   2a) to sleep normally
   2b) euphemistically, to be dead
   2c) metaph.
       2c1) to yield to sloth and sin
       2c2) to be indifferent to one's salvation

—Thayer's (New Testament Greek-English Lexicon)

From G2596 and εὕδω [[heudo]] (to sleep); to lie down to rest, that is, (by implication) to fall asleep (literally or figuratively):—(be a-) sleep.
