  #0582 אֱנוֹשׁ 'enowsh {en-oshe'}

 from H0605; TWOT - 136a; n m

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 1) man, mortal man, person, mankind
    1a) of an individual
    1b) men (collective)
    1c) man, mankind

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

From H0605; properly a mortal (and thus differeing from the more dignified H0120); hence a man in general (singly or collectively). It is often unexpressed in the English Version, especially when used in apposition with another word:—another, X [blood-] thirsty, certain, chap [-man], divers, fellow, X in the flower of their age, husband, (certain, mortal) man, people, person, servant, some (X of them), + stranger, those, + their trade. It is often unexpressed in the Engl. version, especially when used in apposition with another word. Compare H0376.
