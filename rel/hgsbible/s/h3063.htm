  #3063 יְהוּדָה Y@huwdah {yeh-hoo-daw'}

 from H3034; TWOT - 850c; n pr m

—Hebrew Word Study (Transliteration-Pronunciation Etymology & Grammar)


 Judah = "praised"
 1) the son of Jacob by Leah
 2) the tribe descended from Judah the son of Jacob
 3) the territory occupied by the tribe of Judah
 4) the kingdom comprised of the tribes of Judah and Benjamin which
    occupied the southern part of Canaan after the nation split upon
    the death of Solomon
 5) a Levite in Ezra's time
 6) an overseer of Jerusalem in the time of Nehemiah
 7) a Levite musician in the time of Nehemiah
 8) a priest in the time of Nehemiah

—Brown-Driver-Briggs (Old Testament Hebrew-English Lexicon)

From H3034; celebrated; Jehudah (or Judah), the name of five Israelites; also of the tribe descended from the first, and of its territory:—Judah.
